About

The Graph Machine Learning Research Group at the University of Kurdistan is dedicated to advancing the field of artificial intelligence through innovative research on graph-structured data and deep learning techniques. Our interdisciplinary team focuses on developing novel algorithms and models that leverage the complex relationships inherent in graph data to solve real-world problems across various domains such as social networks, bioinformatics, recommendation systems, and more. Committed to excellence in both theoretical foundations and practical applications, we strive to push the boundaries of machine learning by exploring new methodologies that improve accuracy, scalability, and interpretability.

Publications

Our 10 selected publications sorted by most recent.

On the Regularization of Learnable Embeddings for Time Series Processing

Preprint  •  Spatiotemporal data, Graph neural networks, Regularization, Transfer learning, Global-local models

In processing multiple time series, accounting for the individual features of each sequence can be challenging. Modern deep learning methods combine global and local layers, often implemented as learnable embeddings. This paper investigates regularization methods for these embeddings, improving performance and transferability.

Learning Latent Graph Structures and their Uncertainty

Preprint  •  Graph structure learning, Graph neural networks, Model calibration

This paper shows that minimizing point-prediction loss does not guarantee proper learning of latent relational information. It proposes a sampling-based method for joint learning of graph structure and uncertainty.

Temporal Graph ODEs for Irregularly-Sampled Time Series

International Joint Conferences on Artificial Intelligence, 2024  •  Spatiotemporal graphs, Graph neural networks, Irregular sampling

Introduces the Temporal Graph Ordinary Differential Equation framework to learn temporal and spatial dynamics from irregularly sampled graph streams, achieving state-of-the-art results.

Graph-based Virtual Sensing from Sparse and Partial Multivariate Observations

International Conference on Learning Representations, 2024  •  Spatiotemporal graphs, Graph neural networks, Imputation, Graph structure learning

Proposes a graph deep learning method to infer signals at unmonitored locations by exploiting dependencies between correlated variables, outperforming state-of-the-art virtual sensing techniques.

Graph-based Forecasting with Missing Data through Spatiotemporal Downsampling

International Conference on Machine Learning, 2024  •  Spatiotemporal graphs, Forecasting

Addresses forecasting with missing data by hierarchical spatiotemporal downsampling and interpretable attention mechanisms, outperforming existing methods on benchmarks.

Graph Deep Learning for Time Series Forecasting

Preprint  •  Spatiotemporal graphs, Forecasting

Reviews graph-based deep learning methods for correlated time series forecasting, discussing architectures, design principles, and future research directions.

Graph Representation Learning (special session at ESANN 2023)

ESANN, 2023  •  Graph neural networks

Summarizes fundamental concepts and challenges in graph representation learning and contributions accepted for the ESANN 2023 special session.

A Survey on Graph Neural Networks for Time Series: Forecasting, Classification, Imputation, and Anomaly Detection

IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence, 2024  •  Spatiotemporal graphs, Graph neural networks, Forecasting, Imputation, Anomaly detection

Comprehensive review of graph neural networks for time series analysis covering forecasting, classification, anomaly detection, and imputation.

Graph-based Time Series Clustering for End-to-End Hierarchical Forecasting

International Conference on Machine Learning, 2024  •  Spatiotemporal graphs, Forecasting, Time series clustering, Pooling

Proposes a graph-based method to unify relational and hierarchical inductive biases for hierarchical time series forecasting using trainable graph pooling.

Feudal Graph Reinforcement Learning

Transactions on Machine Learning Research, 2024  •  Reinforcement learning, Relational inductive biases, Graph neural networks

Introduces a hierarchical reinforcement learning framework with pyramidal message-passing architecture to tackle composable control problems.
